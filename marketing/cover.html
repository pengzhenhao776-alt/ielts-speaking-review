The History of Glass
From our earliest origins, humans have made use of natural glass. Obsidian — a form of volcanic glass — was used by early civilizations to create cutting tools and weapons. The earliest man-made glass objects, mainly non-transparent glass beads, are thought to date back to around 3500 BC, with finds in Egypt and Eastern Mesopotamia.
In the first century BC, the glass blowing technique was invented along the Syro-Palestinian coast, revolutionizing the glass industry. For the first time, glass could be produced quickly and cheaply on a large scale.

The Romans further developed glass-making techniques. By the end of the first century AD, it had become a common material throughout the Roman Empire. The discovery of clear glass (through the addition of manganese oxide) in the first century AD was another major breakthrough, as it allowed glass to be used for windows for the first time.
